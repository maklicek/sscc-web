SSCC – SolarSpace Carbon Cycle Integrated energy infrastructure for long-term stability What is SSCC SSCC is an integrated energy infrastructure concept designed for systems with a high share of variable renewable energy. It connects: • renewable electricity, • stable thermal sources, • chemical energy carriers, • and predictive control into a single, coordinated system. The goal is not to maximize instantaneous efficiency, but to ensure energy availability over time under real physical and grid constraints. ________________________________________ The problem SSCC addresses Modern energy systems face three structural challenges: • Temporal mismatch between generation and demand (energy is produced when it is not needed, and missing when it is) • Grid limitations (curtailment, congestion, and reduced operational flexibility) • Lack of long-term storage (batteries work in hours, not days or weeks) SSCC addresses these challenges at the system level, not by adding isolated components. ________________________________________ How SSCC works (high level) SSCC operates across multiple time scales: • Seconds to hours Fast stabilization and protection (typically supported by BESS) • Hours to days Thermal storage and load balancing • Days to weeks (or longer) Chemical energy carriers (e.g. hydrogen, ammonia) as long-term reserves Energy is converted, stored, and released based on predicted conditions, not only on instantaneous availability. ________________________________________ BESS and SSCC – complementary roles Battery energy storage systems (BESS) and SSCC are not competing solutions. • BESS provides fast response and short-term stability • SSCC provides long-term availability and resilience Combined, they form a layered system where: • batteries handle speed, • SSCC handles time, • and control logic coordinates both. ________________________________________ Operational scenarios Renewable surplus Excess energy is absorbed, converted, and stored instead of curtailed. Long-term deficit Stored thermal and chemical energy maintains continuity of supply. Grid outage or crisis mode The system enters a degraded but controlled operating state, preserving critical functions and buying time for recovery. ________________________________________ Design philosophy SSCC is based on a few explicit principles: • systems must be stable, not perfect • decisions must be predictable and explainable • long-term resilience is more important than short-term optimization SSCC is not a universal solution, but a systemic framework for regions, industry, and critical infrastructure. ________________________________________ For experts Detailed technical documentation, system architecture, and operational logic are currently available in Czech.
